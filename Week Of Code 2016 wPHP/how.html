HOW TO RECYCLE?


  • Green Bin
    This multi-purpose bin takes tins, cans, paper, cardboard, clean aluminium foil (for example foil trays) drinks cartons, envelopes, shredded paper, plastic bottles and bottle tops, pots, tubs and trays. Please put the items inside the bin loose, do not use plastic or black bags to cover them.

  • Brown Bin
    This garden waste bin takes flowers, plants, garden weeds, grass cuttings, hedge clippings, twigs, small branches and trees cut down to fit in bin.

  • Grey Bin
    This landfill bin is for all waste that can't be recycled e.g. nappies, animal waste and polystyrene and mixed rubbish.

  • Packaging Recycling Point
    Items suitable for recycling in this bin are labelled on the front as they may vary from bin to bin.

  • Food Waste Bin
    Items to recycle in this bin are labelled on the front as they may vary from bin to bin.

  • General Waste Bin
    For all non-recyclable items.

  • Glass Recycling Box
    This box is for all glass (you don't have to remove metal or plastic lids on) , batteries, small electricals and textiles.

  • Streetside Bin
    For all waste on the go.

  • Household Food Bin
    All types of food, including uncooked and cooked, excluding liquids like cooking oil and milk. Bag your waste in compostable bags or old plastic bags. You can buy compostable liners for your food waste caddy at all libraries for Â£1 a roll.

  • Skip
    For all construction or large waste eg. furniture, staircases and industrial (not chemical) waste.

  • Industrial Paper Bin
    Can also be used for residential paper disposal as well as envelopes, newspaper, magazines and more labelled on front.

  • Internal Office Bin
    These bins are labbelled on the front and vary by office.
