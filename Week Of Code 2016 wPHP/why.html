Recycling

What is it?

When you filter your waste so less goes to landfill, helping the planet, securing the future and ensuring many more years with the only earth we will ever get. Does this page look like too much? See the quick (and penguin) version by clicking here

Benefits for industries

Recycling saves energy because the manufacturer doesn't have to produce something new from raw natural resources. By using recycled materials we save on energy consumption, which keeps production costs down. Recycling reduces the need for more landfills freeing up more valuable, limited space.

Benefits for the planet

Landfill and general waste is a major contributor to global warming. Solid waste landfills are the single largest man-made source of methane gas. Methane (CH4) is a greenhouse gas that is 23 times more effective at trapping heat in the atmosphere than the most common greenhouse gas, carbon dioxide (CO2). In one year, 43 per cent of all penguin chick deaths were attributed to climate change and in another 50 per cent. Starvation and the weather were likely to interact increasingly as the climate changed, according to the researchers. 65 per cent of penguin chicks died each year, with 40 per cent starving (Daily Mail). And these harms do not just affect penguins, they affect all other species on earth as well as humans too. It's estimated up to 75% (GreenWaste) of landfill could have been recycled with only 30% of it actually recycled. Recycling helps our planet; helping our home, our planet and our beloved penguins.

Sources: Edinburgh City Council, Scottish Enviromental Protection Agency , Google, Oxford University, New York Times, LiveScience, Daily Mail
